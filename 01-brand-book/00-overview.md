## What this is

A new portfolio site for Kashfi Rashid (Kash). Working name: `portfolio2026`.

It's not a CV. It's not a project gallery. It's a small world that visitors enter, get named inside of, get spoken to inside of, and leave a mark on if they want to. The work is in there — projects, process, people — but the work is not the point. The point is the feeling of having been somewhere specific.

If a stranger lands on the site for thirty seconds and closes the tab, the one feeling that should follow them out is: *this guy actually thinks about people.* Not "professional." Not "creative." That specific feeling.

---

## The line

**Ambitious but executioneery.**

Kash's own phrase, pulled directly from chat. It's the candidate for the homepage hero, the companion's first words, or a section header — most likely all three in different forms.

It sounds slightly off, on purpose. *Executioneery* isn't a word. That's the point. Most people are ambitious *or* execute. Kash is both, and the awkward portmanteau holds that contradiction. **Do not replace this with something more polished.** A "professional" rewrite ("ambitious and executes" / "ideas to execution" / "from concept to ship") would erase the brand. The misshape is the signal.

---

## Why this site needs to exist

Kash already has a portfolio at kashfirashid.com. It works as a CV. It does not work as Kash.

The current site reads as competent and generic. *"From pixels to pull requests, I keep ideas moving."* That sentence could appear on ten thousand other portfolios without changing a word. The new site replaces that with something that could only appear on Kash's.

There's also a competitive context: two of Kash's references (Harjot Singh, Keyaan Vegdani) are both at SFU SIAT, both graduating around the same time. The new site has to differentiate sharply. The differentiator is not "more polished" — it's "more specifically Kash."

---

## The wow stack — three composed layers

The site combines three references, each doing one job. None dominates. The wow is the composition.

1. **Visitor identity** — borrowed from Harjot Singh. Visitor enters their name on first visit, gets assigned a color, their cursor becomes that color. They become a named character inside the site, not just a viewer.
2. **Companion** — evolved from Abdul Hamoui. Cursor-attached chat bubbles that surface short, contextual annotations when the visitor hovers, clicks, scrolls past, or goes idle. Not a chatbot — visitors don't type to it. The companion is a version of Kash that lives in cursor space and speaks rarely but specifically.
3. **Personal world** — borrowed from Keyaan Vegdani. The site has a setting, not just a layout. Setting: **2am studio environment.** Late-night working aesthetic — monitor glow, cursor traces, ambient sound where it fits, screen recordings of Kash actually working, the textures of the time of day when the real work happens.

A fourth, lighter layer comes from Caleb Wu: **return-visit reward.** The companion remembers visitors by name and color across sessions. Different idle reels surface on second and third visits.

A fifth, structural layer comes from Dropbox brand site: **modular sections.** Eight rooms instead of three CV tabs. The site is a system to explore, not a page to scroll.

## What makes this brand different from every other designer portfolio

Most designer portfolios in 2026 lean into one of three tropes: the polished case-study grid, the maximalist motion-art flex, or the AI-generated-looking shimmer. This one rejects all three.

The differentiators, plainly:

- **Voice over polish.** The copy reads warm-reflective and direct, not corporate-confident. *"I plan things to death before I touch the keyboard. it's a problem and a feature."* lands harder than *"Driven by curiosity and a love for clean, intentional design."*
- **Process as content, not as a section.** Kash orchestrates Antigravity, Stitch, Gemini, Claude (chat, cowork, code) — that conducting is itself part of the work. The Process section *shows* it happening, not describes it after.
- **People structurally proof-able.** Every "I think about people" claim has a named, specific moment behind it: Sazzad's refugee letter, Tarif's LinkedIn, the IT Squad incident triage, Tazwar's ENGL101 voice match. Abstract claims get hard-linked to concrete proof, Abdul-Hamoui-style.
- **Hall of Fame instead of contact form.** Visitors leave design feedback. The good ones get implemented and credited publicly. The site visibly evolves because of who came through it. That replaces the standard "let's grab a coffee" closer.
- **Companion as quiet recognition.** Visitors get named, colored, and clocked when they come back. Most portfolios deliver content; this one delivers a small relationship. ← v1.1 *(was: "the layer of intimacy" — replaced, too literary for the register)*
- **Restraint as the visual baseline.** Seido and Spendy/FutureSpend are the bar — not Awwwards-flashy. The 2am studio world favors quiet glow over saturated color, ambient motion over auto-playing bounce, screen recordings over Lottie loaders.
- **The misshape stays.** *Ambitious but executioneery* doesn't get sanded down into a polished tagline. The roughness is the brand.

## Who this brand is for

The site speaks to three audiences without contorting itself for any of them:

- **Hiring managers and recruiters** evaluating Kash for design / design-engineering / creative-tech roles. They get the work, the range, and the receipts. Not in CV form — in a way that's harder to forget.
- **Peer designers and developers** scanning portfolios for craft and taste. They get the references, the process, the orchestration, the fact that he reads visual restraint better than most. They leave thinking *huh, this person has taste.*
- **Friends, family, and people who already know Kash** who want a place that feels like him. They get the warm-reflective voice, the helping-people moments, the small jokes the companion makes, the secret footer.

What the site is **not** for: brand managers looking for stock-photo professionalism, agencies wanting a portfolio in a familiar format, anyone who needs a printable resume. There's a Resume link if asked. The site itself doesn't bend toward those uses.

## How to use this brand book

For Kash:

- Read in order. Most files are short.
- Redline anything that sounds wrong. v1 is a draft, not a verdict.
- The open-questions list in `README.md` shows which decisions are still blocking which phases. Answer those in order of leverage.

For designers / developers joining later:

- Read this file, then `01-voice.md`, then `06-visual-direction.md`. Everything else is reference for when you need it.
- When in doubt, default to restraint. Seido and Spendy/FutureSpend are the visual standard. Anything more decorated than those references is probably wrong.
- The companion is the easiest thing to get wrong. Re-read `04-companion-spec.md` before writing a single bubble. Bubbles are short, contextual, and in Kash's voice. Not friendly-customer-service voice. Not Clippy.

---

## What this brand book is not

It's not a website. It's the source of truth that exists *before* the website. Wireframes (`/02-wireframes/`), hi-fi design (`/03-design/`), and build (`/04-build/`) all reference this folder. If a future decision contradicts something here, this folder gets updated first, then the implementation follows. Otherwise the brand drifts, and drift is how a site ends up reading polished-corporate three months in.
