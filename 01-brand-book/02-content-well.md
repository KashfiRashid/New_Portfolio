## 9. Opinions — design takes Kash will defend

The Voice section publishes 5–10 opinions. These are the takes Kash will argue for. Starter list — Kash refines in `questions-for-kash.md` C1. **Voice register: declarative title + 2–3 sentence body that lands. No three-paragraph essays.**

Confirmed from chat (titles only — body lands when Kash writes it):

- **Restraint reads as confidence.** Subtraction is the harder skill. A site that doesn't show everything it can do is the one that gets remembered.
- **AI-generated-looking UI is bad. Use AI to direct, not to generate.** The conducting is the work. The output isn't.
- **Process is content. Show the messy middle.** A polished case study is a story; a working notebook is a portfolio.
- **Plan more than you think you should. Then plan a little more.** Plans before keys. It's slower at the start and faster everywhere else.
- **Most portfolios are CVs in disguise. This one isn't.** Hall of Fame replaces the contact form for a reason.
- **AI doesn't make you faster. It makes you wider.** The hours don't shrink. The number of directions you can hold open at once does. That's a different superpower.
- **Late nights aren't a flex; they're a workspace.** Most of what Kash makes happens at 2am. That's not a brag, it's just where the focus shows up.
- **People over deliverables.** Every project here has a name attached to a person who shaped it. The portfolio is a list of names as much as a list of works.
