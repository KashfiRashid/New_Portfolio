> Dhaka → Delta. Selective Bangla. The page that shows where Kash is from without doing diaspora-by-template. Ends near the convocation moment (June 10, 2026).

## Purpose

- Make Origin specific, not generic-diaspora
- Use Bangla selectively (headers, pull-quote, possibly the convocation note)

### OPENING PARAGRAPH

3–5 sentences. Concrete details. Specific year, specific moment.

[NEEDS KASH INPUT — Q B/C territory] full text needed. Working principle: open with a sensory moment, not biographical exposition.

Bad opening (don't ship):
> "I was born in Dhaka, Bangladesh, and moved to Canada in 2022 to study at SFU."

> *"the day in 2022 i landed at YVR my dad was holding two coffees. one for each of us. i hadn't had a coffee in 17 hours and i didn't remember telling him my order. it was right."*

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ A map graphic with arrows from Bangladesh → Canada. Visualization-of-a-cliché.
- ❌ A timeline component with year markers. Reads as resume.
- ❌ "About me" framing anywhere on this page. Origin is its own thing.
- ❌ Generic diaspora language — *"finding my voice between two cultures,"* *"bridging worlds."* The page either has Kash's specific story or it shouldn't exist.
- ❌ Bangla used decoratively (random words sprinkled in for "flavor"). Bangla is selective and intentional — see `06-visual-direction.md` and `00-overview.md`.
- ❌ A "where I'm from" carousel of cities with statistics.
- ❌ Photos of food. (No matter how Bengali the food is, the cliché is real.)

---

## What earns this page

A working Origin page passes this test: someone from Dhaka reads it and thinks *"this is real,"* and someone who knows nothing about Bangladesh reads it and thinks *"that was specific, not exotic."* Both readings are required.

The risk on this page is highest: get the voice wrong here and it reads as either confessional-overshare or as polite-diaspora-template. Tazwar register threads the needle — warm, specific, never performative.
